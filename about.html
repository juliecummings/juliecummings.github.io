A little bit about myself.

Let us start with the basics. I started my journey as an engineer as I am analytical and have liked mathematics since young. However, I could not shake the urge to draw, paint, and make things. I wanted — NEEDED — to create. Hence, I got a diploma in Graphic Design because of my fascination with technology and desire to understand ‘Art’ such as the anatomy of type, colour palettes and theory, logo design, proportions, and so on.

The idea to progress into User Experience Design came mainly as a result of my curiosity with (the always advancing) technology and (the accompanying never entirely understood) psychology. Discovering UX design was the most natural next adventure into design, technology growth and human behaviour (psychology evolution).

I am an analytical and determined UX Designer who is passionate about the power and role of technology in people’s lives and why so. For me, the core magic of UX design is in truly understanding people’s needs and based on that, build systems and products that will positively impact their lives. Its beauty and marvel comes with an aesthetically pleasing UI that hits all the right interactions and users are blissfully unaware of life before it.

A bit more about myself… I am of mixed heritage — being that my dad is English and my mum is Chinese. I grew up being exposed to both cultures. As such, I can speak fluent English and Mandarin. School holiday trips back to Cornwall aside, I spent most of my formative years in Singapore (a tiny but vastly multicultural country). I believe that my ability to quickly adapt and adjust to new challenges in life comes from my diverse upbringing. Keenness to learn more about people and their different ways of living is another side effect. Crafting, books, lots of tea, and tinkering are also an innate part of who I am.
