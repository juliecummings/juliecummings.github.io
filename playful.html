Playful Interactions

Playful interactions are sources of emotion — potentially making the user feel, for example, stimulated, challenged, curious, proud, frustrated, happy, confused or sad. Some might say that playful interactions is the essence of video games. In this topic, we are more specifically looking at how fun can change our reactions and behaviours towards going through mundane tasks.

If this interests you, take a look at examples I found in my research, a mini conceptual assignment and my views on the topic.

Research on Playful Interactions

Playful interactions, in this context, essentially involves the fun theory. Put simply, if you want to get people to change their behaviour, make what you want them to do novel and fun. The Fun Theory was an initiative of Volkswagen in 2009-2010 that was meant to acknowledge the thoughts, ideas and inventions that “proved” fun is the easiest way of changing behavior for the better.

There have been numerous attempts with this but the most prominent ones are as shared:


As I was going through the array of experiments conducted by students and companies, I realize that they all tend to target a change in actions that are ordinarily dull and repetitive. Instances like commuting, disposing on waste or even grocery shopping. I suppose that is it is most effective to spark amusement. There are also a couple that are targeted at creating sustainable change like recycling. Examples include drink-o, bottle bank arcade, whack-a-can recycle machine and COFFEE BIN.

Whilst idea generation in this theme can be plentiful, I wanted to delve deeper into those that I personally found interesting. Whilst looking at what it can mean for users and society as a whole, I am also curious as to why these fun-inciting playful interactions are not developed further.

DISCUSSION:

WAITING AT TRAFFIC LIGHTS

What could be a more mundane, dull and repetitive notion than waiting at traffic junctions for the light to turn green. As such, playful interactions incorporated to make it more entertaining in such a situation was attempted multiple times. Two of which I found particularly intriguing.

If you prefer, you can read up on the dancing traffic light or streetpong.
Otherwise, the following videos depict how the concepts work when applied in real life.

With the dancing traffic light, I cannot help but smile as I watched the video. People were enjoying participating /dancing but others were also amused with watching it mirrored in the traffic light. On the other hand, streetpong is only targeted at one individual on each side and is reliant on someone on the other side of the road wanting to play along. Due to this, street pong should only be implemented on busy crossings to prevent having no one of the other side of the road. However, targeting only one player on each side at busy intersections seems counter productive. On the other hand, the dancing traffic light's novelty is fleeting and so is the concept. With streetpong, there is at least some variation as with your opponent.

Regardless, both have the purpose of entertaining its users while the dancing traffic light amuses not just the user but everyone in the vicinity as well. However, I am fascinated with these two playful interactions because I find myself pondering over whether there is some way they can evolve with time to retain its wonder and novelty or are playful interactions always meant to be ephemeral?
