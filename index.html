About

Hey! I am Julie. I am English but I was born in Singapore. English is my native language but ... 我的中文也不错.

I am currently studying, in my second year of User Experience Design in The Hague University of Applied Sciences, in the Netherlands.

I like to make things work.
I want to make an impression.

This website serves as a portfolio for the course I am undertaking now, Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) Tecnhologies, and is a way of keeping track of my contributions. Learning to research and engaging in several preset assignments on HCI technologies will aid in the development of interactive concepts during the lab weeks.

I expect this course to be different. Whilst User Experience can still come into play, I welcome the more hands-on approach for a change. I also anticipate there to be numerous struggles but also fun tasks. Although I am not the best with technology, I enjoy the challenge. I shall take pride and be amazed at what I can end up doing.

I imagine this course will give chances for me to practise and improve on my programming capabilites. Plus, I have always been enthusiastic about physics. As such, I hope that I get to learn as well as play with circuitry and create wonderful things with them. All in all, I want to fathom the possibilities new technologies can create for future designs by understanding what it can accomplish for design now.

Excursion
For an excursion, we visited NEMO science museum in Amsterdam.

The creative mind behind NEMO, built in 1997, is Italian architect Renzo Piano. He had a clear vision for his design of the NEMO building, with its very own piazza 22 metres above water level. The spectacular roof terrace has a magnificent view over Amsterdam and the Eastern Docklands.

NEMO SCIENCE MUSEUM IN AMSTERDAM
On the banks of the river IJ lies the futuristic NEMO: the Walhalla for every curious soul who likes to experiment. The building has the shape of a giant green ship and is clearly recognizable from afar. NEMO Science Museum introduces the world of science and technology using a variety of playful interactions / exhibitions. The museum calls itself a science centre, but that does not mean that lay people have nothing to look for — on the contrary! NEMO makes science and technology fun, exciting and accessible. One visit to this centre and physics suddenly doesn't seem that complicated anymore.

Fenomena ⋆ Technium ⋆ Elementa ⋆ Humania ⋆ Energetica
Discover how things work with five-storeys of NEMO. No dull lessons, but doing, seeing, feeling and experiencing. Lock yourself in a self-blown bubble, cycle over a cable three meters in the air, explore your DNA with a 3D journey through your body and get to know Electra, Europe's largest human robot.




Lab Weeks

Lab Weeks consists of two weeks / nine days in which we work continuously and intensively on our interactive concept in groups of five students. Doing so in a location outside of the Haagse Hogeschool (at Maakhaven) gives us a special space to put us in a mindframe to fully focus on devising and building our interactive installation. Each of us were asked to select our preferences on the four topics and groups were formed taking this into account. Within the topic, we were tasked with researching new concepts, applications, interaction forms, user experiences and a whole lot of 'tinkering'. These interactive installations had to be designed in such a way that they can be presented in an exhibition with which we conclude these lab weeks.

Arduino Assignments

Arduino is an open-source electronics platform based on easy-to-use hardware and software. It is intended for artists, designers and anyone interested in creating interactive objects or environments. Arduino consists of an open source micro-controller and a software environment to program it in. Arduino projects can run stand-alone (without a computer), but can also communicate with software that runs on a computer (eg Processing, MaxMSP). Arduino boards are able to read inputs - light on a sensor, a finger on a button, or a Twitter message - and turn it into an output - activating a motor, turning on an LED, publishing something online.
