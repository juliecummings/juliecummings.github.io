About

Hey! I am Julie. I am English but I was born in Singapore. English is my native language but ... 我的中文也不错.

I am currently studying, in my second year of User Experience Design in The Hague University of Applied Sciences, in the Netherlands.

I like to make things work.
I want to make an impression.

This website serves as a portfolio for the course I am undertaking now, Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) Tecnhologies, and is a way of keeping track of my contributions. Learning to research and engaging in several preset assignments on HCI technologies will aid in the development of interactive concepts during the lab weeks.

I expect this course to be different. Whilst User Experience can still come into play, I welcome the more hands-on approach for a change. I also anticipate there to be numerous struggles but also fun tasks. Although I am not the best with technology, I enjoy the challenge. I shall take pride and be amazed at what I can end up doing.

I imagine this course will give chances for me to practise and improve on my programming capabilites. Plus, I have always been enthusiastic about physics. As such, I hope that I get to learn as well as play with circuitry and create wonderful things with them. All in all, I want to fathom the possibilities new technologies can create for future designs by understanding what it can accomplish for design now.

Programming
April 2018

This course focuses on programming for the interactive web. It aims to teach knowledge and understanding of the logic and structure required in programming for the web. The approach to programming in the UXD-program is that programming is an essential skill during each part of the design process. It is used along side other techniques, to stimulate creativity, validate design solutions and communicate resulting products. Therefore, the program focuses on creating programmed sketches, proof of concepts and prototypes respectively.

ASSIGNMENT 1

The assignment is to design and create a website that is to be used inside the spacecraft. The purpose of the website is to give information about the trip and to prepare travellers for upcoming arrival on mars. Factors can include ship controls, ship environment, supplies or anything else but these travellers should be able to use the interface anywhere on the ship. My inspiration is the movie, ‘Passengers’. Hence, the first things that came to mind was a list of activities, food and drinks. However, I realized that if I were a passenger on this voyage, there are a few updates I would need to know that takes precedence over those stated above. Namely, the current date, time and distance to the destination (it takes 6 to 8 months!) and perhaps, some outlet to voice my concerns. Plus, I feel that paying $200,000 for a ticket deems a classier and polite tone. With this and the factors in mind, I made a rough HTML and CSS to see what I can work with. I tried to play the DOM by calling IDs which got me carried away giving IDs to everything. When I got to the countdown, I thought how annoying it would be to keep having to look at the long remaining time. Hence, I felt like this is a good extra opportunity to work with timers. By looking up forms, I found an option of putting a field as ‘required’ but did not manage to find a way the forms can possibly be sent by email. Also, I wanted to make a list of activities that can be checked or cancelled off like ‘take a swim’, etc. However, I was unsure of how to create an overlay or modal boxes.

ASSIGNMENT 2

The second assignment is to design and create a large animated Space Clock to be projected onto the dome which would serve as a central timepiece. The style and theme is open but actual time and date should be shown in an interesting way. I started off with a flip clock in mind as they always amuse me and I could watch it for minutes. However, examples I found were either done using jQuery or FlipClock.js and I decided it was too difficult. Hence, I began with an analog clock with the aid of the tutorial whilst adding my own spin. I got engrossed in SVGs. The same instructor had two interesting videos on them. With the aid of ‘Learning SVG’ and a browse of ‘Creating Web Icons with SVG’ by Morten RandHendriksen, I made my own SVG and added the date on top of it. I really wished SVG was taught in the lectures though. As I had an extra week to work on this, I tried making a simpler flip clock without gradients or shadows. Also, GreenSock platform had examples. In one called ‘GreenSock Homepage Animation’ they rotated a square box 180degrees which gives the same look. In the end, it was my two separate HTML, CSS and JavaScript that proved tricky to merge. Things kept going missing. Additionally, I really should have stuck with my plan for the analog clock as I would love to have seen that in actualization. I wanted the inner shadow and an external long shadow to rotate along with the hour arm between 10am-10pm. Outside of those times, the inner face would be grey and there would not be a long shadow.

Check my clocks out!



ASSIGNMENT 3

The third and last assignment is about 32 colonists are returning to their home planet, Earth, from mars. We were to design and create a means to find the perfect landing spot on here on the Earth surface. There are many factors to take into account when landing on our surface. I decided to incorporate clouds or general weather conditions, previews of potential landing spots as images and marking possible landing areas on the map. I started off by mapping where I want my map. Firstly, I aimed to keep the page as a one-page dashboard (without scrolling). Hence, I decided the map should only take up half the width. Google maps are able to zoom in and open in full screen anyway. Next step was making the Google Maps API to work. Whilst I like playing with styles, the style of the night mode was beautiful so I incorporated some of the colours from it to the rest of my page. However, the colour of the water was a sight of sore eye. Thus, I made it black. As I am assuming home is England or its thereabouts, I did not want the open Weather API to take any input. I decided to set my own instead. Rather than seeing them all at the time, I made it such that you only display the current weather when clicked on. Plus, it disappears after 10 seconds (I cannot get enough of this). The last thing I did was the information windows. They were trickier than I thought they would be. I ended up having a very messy content line as the content of the information window has to be placed in JavaScript and that is very strange to me.

Check my map and weather API out!
