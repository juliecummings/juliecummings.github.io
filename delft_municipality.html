DELFT MUNICIPALITY

Tool to empower children to actively participate with the placemaking of public spaces

Clickable Prototype »

CLIENT

Gemeente Delft (as it is known by locals) is the municipality of the medium-sized city of Delft in the Netherlands. This city has over 100,000 inhabitants and lies between 2 of the 4 largest Dutch cities, Rotterdam and The Hague. In 2019, the city decided to invest in student driven projects in a bid to becoming a smarter city.
As a team, we had to pitch to earn the case we want and each case had its own product owner and specific department within the municipality.

CHALLENGE

The city of Delft wants the help of all citizens in the development of the city in general. The issue more specifically is in the design of public spaces. Children and young adults are a difficult to address target group. Therefore, the objective is to design an attractive ‘placemaking’ tool suitable for said target group.
Bonus would be managing to involve the focal target group of students at Technasium Christelijk Lyceum Delft.

MY ROLE

In this project, I worked agile in collaboration alongside 4 other UX design students while using the scrum methodology. Over 3 sprints, I worked in different phases of the design process which includes research through design, defining, concepting, prototyping, and user testing in each of the sprints.

The client’s end goal was to activate, engage and co-create with children (as well as young adults) in the design of Delft’s public spaces with an experiential placemaking tool for children certified by children.

SPRINT ONE RESEARCH AND CONCEPTUAL

To kickstart this project, I pitched our direction to the product owners to earn the case. In a nutshell, our direction was a three-prong approach — Activation, Engagement , and Co-Creation — as described in an excerpt of the pitch deck below. This sprint started with thorough Desk Research and Observation. Knowledge gathered was then brought forth into a co-creation session with the Team and the first experiential prototype was made. The tool has three aspects — ‘ Getting into the role (Police, Alien, and Artist)’, ‘Building your dream city’, and ‘A story in your city’ — to gather a broader range of reception from the target audience.

SPRINT TWO RESEARCH AND CONCEPTUAL

Sprint 2 begun with a session plan being drawn up to see what additional materials would be necessary to achieve our aim of getting insights on how children experience building their own space. Also, it assists us to better facilitate the session with the intended users.

The experiential prototype from the end of the previous sprint was adapted slightly based on key insights gathered during the co-creation session with stakeholders. Plus, as we were conducting Research through Design — a simple Feedback Form was made and an Assumption List was prepared to aid in our targeted in-field observation.

THE SOLUTION HIGHLIGHTS

The design empowers children (as well as young adults) to actively engage in the design of Delft’s public spaces by co-creating with an experiential placemaking tool specifically crafted for them through testing with groups of children (young adults).

The final solution combines a phone application with physical and tactile components like stickers, a card deck, and markers.

(LEFT) QR STICKERS HIDDEN ON LOCATION

Encouraging children (with friends) to actively revisit locations that are in the placemaking initiative. Additionally, hiding said stickers provides a fun aspect to the process. Those greater in “power” are more well-hidden.

(RIGHT) PORTABLE PACK OF CARDS

A deck of cards is first and foremost small and light, making it easier for children to carry it along with them. The pack of cards includes 26 blank cards and 26 randomised cards. The latter is to engage children to trade with others for pre-made cards of their preference — fostering interaction. Plus, the deck comes with two random coloured markers and an insert to provide a simple onboarding process for the children.

ONBOARDING

Our research show instructions are important to our users so clear and concise onboarding for core features are included.

PLACING GNOMES

To instill a cheeky fun element, users collect gnomes as time passes which they can place in the location designs of others.

ELIMINATING GNOMES

To eliminate gnomes, the app brings users back on location to search for hidden stickers that gives them weapons to use.

EDITING SPACES & CUSTOM OWNERSHIP

Locations that are in play for the Delft municipality pops up on a map for users to edit.
The app allows our target users custom ownership of the space they created by letting them name it and add a little rumour.

GALLERY (LOCATION) & SORTING OPTIONS

Children can easily gallery view creations of their peers.
The sorting feature empowers users to browse most liked edits as well as creations that have the most gnome overcrowding.

NOTIFICATIONS & SENDING INVITES

In-app notifications provides a way for users to keep updated without it being intrusive.
The feature to send invites for placemaking talents was added to aid the Delft municipality’s goal of hosting sessions.
