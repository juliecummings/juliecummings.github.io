HEEMBOUW

Service design to encourage activity based working and sustainable behaviour

Clickable Prototype »

CLIENT

Heembouw designs and builds “spaces where people want to be”. Heembouw is committed to creating living as well as working environments where people feel at home.

CHALLENGE

Heembouw needed a solution for activity based working as their current layout did not function optimally. Plus, the client aimed to promote a healthy work style based on the WELL standard. Finally, Heembouw needed to make their employees aware of the role they each play in the sustainability of the building.

MY ROLE

In this project, I worked in collaboration alongside 3 other UX design students. I worked in different phases of the design process which includes research, defining, concepting, prototyping, user testing, and videography.

The client’s goal was to stimulate the employees of Heembouw to use the various available work spaces in such a way that supports the well-being of the employees whilst having a positive environmental impact.

RESEARCH

After conducting an extensive research and analysing all the gathered data from the diverse utilised methods such as interviews, observation, ‘a day in the life’, graffiti wall, and behavioural mapping. Visualisations were made to capture what was learnt throughout the research process.

Some key insights from the research were:
  • ★ Employees are unaware of how the initiatives work and their benefits;
  • ★ People yearn for more greenery in the working environment;
  • ★ There is social pressure to be located where others expect you to be;
  • ★ Employees are interested in efficiency: easy and quick-to-do tasks;
  • ★ Having to carry everything around can be a huge deterrence.

CONCEPTUAL

Using the gathered insights, it was time to come up with concepts to solve the problem. After a long ideation process involving the 6-3-5 Brainwriting, Affinity Diagrams, and a Morphological Chart, we have a ‘go-ahead’ from the stakeholders on the first concepts and began to conduct usability tests

For that, we utilised the Desktop Walkthrough technique with a cardboard representation of the space and paper prototypes of the phone application and interactive tablet screen for the first testing.

Subsequently, we used Service Staging to further test the prototypes of our second and third iterations.

THE SOLUTION HIGHLIGHTS

The design empowers employees to make conscious choices in order to optimise activity based working as well as provide ease and awareness of sustainability efforts.

The final solution combines a phone application, interactive tablet, smart trash bin, and tv screen with feedback.

(LEFT) PROVIDE INFORMATION

Informing employees of the activity based working initiative by clearly and distinctly defining the spaces. Additionally, syncing these tablets with the appropriate functionalities in the phone application makes it more feasible.

(RIGHT) BOOKING SPACES

Easy and fast searching and booking of spaces. Employees can book rooms for individual or group projects and add other employees to the booking. As such, the reservation would sync up in real time and appear in the application’s agenda of all the participants. Plus, this can be viewed on location on tablets dedicated to each space.

STORAGE

Secure storage facilities enables the employees to move around light during the day. Hence, using the various work spaces can be carried out with more ease.

FIND AND BE FOUND

The map feature empowers the employees to locate each other and be located at any time of the day. Employees can also call, message or ask other employees to meet them.

PRIVACY

Employees are given the option to hide themselves from the map if wanted. The application allows them to protect their privacy. When users go offline, they are also not able to see the location of other employees.

INSTRUCTIONS AND FEEDBACK

Providing clear instructions for sustainable behaviour. Providing instant feedback and gratification with knowing the impact of sustainable choices.

MOTIVATION AND COMPETITION

Highlighting the contributions as a whole on the already present television screen can incentivise the employees. Also, starting some healthy plant competition can address the employees’ need for more greenery and instill some fun in camaraderie.
